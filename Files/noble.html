Jane Addams

Jane Addams (1860-1935) was a pioneering social worker and activist, best known for co-founding Hull House in Chicago. This settlement house provided vital services and education to a diverse community of immigrants. A dedicated advocate for social justice, she fought for women's suffrage, peace, and public health. For her tireless efforts, Addams became the first American woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize in 1931.

Elie Wiesel

Elie Wiesel (1928-2016) was a Holocaust survivor, writer, and activist. A survivor of Auschwitz and Buchenwald, his memoir Night is a powerful testament to the horrors he endured. Wiesel dedicated his life to combating indifference and injustice, using his voice to ensure the world would never forget the Holocaust. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1986 for his work as a "messenger to mankind."

Kailash Satyarthi

Kailash Satyarthi (born 1954) is an Indian social reformer known for his relentless fight against child labor. He founded the Bachpan Bachao Andolan (Save the Childhood Movement) to rescue and rehabilitate children from exploitation. For his work in promoting the right of all children to education, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014, sharing it with Malala Yousafzai.
